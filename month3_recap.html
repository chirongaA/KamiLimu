Sessions Breakdown üìö

Purpose: Nurture employability, grow responsible innovation, and enhance learning outcomes.

Interview Feedback Factors: Demeanor, clarity, confidence, attitude, problem solving, and structured responses.

STAR method: Tips to answer behavioural/situational questions. SITUATION ‚Üí TASK ‚Üí ACTION ‚Üí RESULT

Present-Past-Future model: Tips to answer ‚ÄúTell us about yourself‚Äù.

Interview Phases: Preparation (doing research, practising technical and behavioural questions), delivery (taking notes, asking the panel questions), post-reflection and feedback.

Networking Essentials: We went through the OSI model, TCP/IP model and IP addressing. We also looked at telnet; a protocol that allows a client to connect and communicate with a remote system and issue commands.

Active Directory: We went through the Windows Domain, Active Directory, Policies and Authentication. In a nutshell, AD is a service that holds the information of all of the objects that exist on your network.

TryHackMe Labs: Networking Concepts, Active Directory Basics

Red vs Blue: Red team focuses on offensive testing and exploitation; Blue team on defense, detection and response.

The wrold of cybersecurity channels: IoT, Quantam computing, Networking, Blockchains, Mobile applications...

Offensive Tools: Which deal with reconnaissance and gathering (How do you know where you are and what is available for attack?). We looked at tools like nmap, burpsuite, metasploit, hydra, netexec.

Defensive Tools: Which deal with detection and information gathering: finding the breach and assessing logs and data for how it was done. Some of the tools mentioned include : SIEM, Wireshark, EDR, IDS/IDP, VirusTotal, backups.

TryHackMe Labs: Offensive Intro, Defensive Intro, Breaching AD

Interview Prep: Doing research about the organization and panelists, Looking into the job description, tailoring your CV to that specific role

5 most frequently asked questions:

  • 1. Tell me about yourself? (It should always be in line with the role you are applying for. Use PPF rule)
  • 2. What are your strengths and weaknesses/ things that would prevent you from being an active contributor? (Relate to some of the keywords used in the JD)
  • 3. How do you handle pressure? (Availing yourself for overtime, breaking down taks, staying calm, prioritizing tasks)
  • 4. Where do you see yourself in x years? (Always respond by saying you wish to grow in that company.)
  • 5. Do you have any questions for us? Always have one: -Do you have learning opportunities for juniors? - What is the typical onboarding process? - What do you need from me to see to know I'm the right person for this role?

Innovation Metrics: Addresses a need, Creates Value, Feasible, User-Friendly, Sustainable, Scalable

What makes a problem worth solving? RELEVANT, SOLVABLE, MARKETABLE

How do we identify a problem? Trends, Observation, Engaging Potential Solutions, Using Deficient Solutions

How do we define a problem? We use the 5 Whys? The idea behind 5 Whys is that by the time we get to that 5th one, we would have specified the problem much better.

Crafting a problem statement:A problem statement summarizes a challenge (the general problem) you want to resolve, its cause (the specific problem), who it impacts (specific target group), and why it‚Äôs important to solve it.

Refining a problem statement: By collecting more information or data from trends, users, observations...
